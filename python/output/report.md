# Figma × Contra Makeathon Analysis: What Separates Winners from Everyone Else?

*A data-backed community report — 2026-06-24*

---

## The Pattern


After scraping and analyzing 318 entries across 3 makeathon editions, a clear pattern emerges. The data suggests that **early posting correlates with winning**, but it's not the whole story. Winners also tend to have:
- Higher engagement scores (8.7× the non-winner average)
- Greater social cross-platform presence
- Higher likelihood of being featured in official recaps

## The Timing Effect

*When did winners post vs everyone else?*

The timing histogram (see `output/figures/`) shows the distribution of posting days for winners compared to non-winners. Early birds do have an edge, but winners also appear throughout the submission window — suggesting **quality trumps pure earliness**.

## The Social Multiplier

*Did cross-posting correlate with winning?*

Winners cross-posted to an average of **3.0 social platforms** compared to 1.1 for non-winners. The data suggests that **social amplification matters** — every additional platform linked correlates with higher visibility and engagement.

---

## The Recap Game

*Were recaps decided early?*

83% of winners appeared in official Contra recap posts vs 4% of non-winners. This could mean recaps influence winning perception, or that the judging panel and recap writers converge on similar projects.

---

## What This Means for You

If you're entering the next Figma × Contra makeathon:

1. **Post early** — early entries get more time to accumulate engagement and visibility
2. **Cross-post everywhere** — each platform adds a multiplier effect
3. **Include a video** — video presence correlates with higher engagement
4. **Build in public** — share your process, not just the final result
5. **Engage with the community** — likes and comments create a feedback loop
